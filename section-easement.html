Easement — Progress After Fifty Years

In progress
Summary

The organization has operated for decades without a recorded easement for access to critical ditch infrastructure. Securing this easement has been identified as a priority since the current board took office. Research into county records has located the original deed containing the easement description and legal centerline. A surveyor will be needed to identify the easement on the ground so that a recorded instrument can be executed with the landowner. This represents the first concrete progress on this issue in the history of the organization.

Supporting Documents
President's Agenda Annual Meeting 2026 — Easement
The following annual meeting agenda, issued by the President, includes a proposed resolution regarding the easement involving the payment of money and the transfer of water rights. This proposal was put forward without any supporting research, title review, or documentation. The easement has been an identified priority since the current board took office five years ago. In that time, no visit to the county recorder's office was made and no title research was conducted by the President. The Treasurer subsequently located the original deed containing the easement description through independent research. Members are encouraged to review the easement section of this site for the full record before considering any proposal that would commit the organization's funds or water rights.

Easement Description & Surveyor Maps
The following documents include the easement description for the Bridgepoint Ditch and the corresponding surveyor maps of the ditch corridor, located through independent research by the Treasurer. These documents were recently presented to landowner Shirley Woodriff in a meeting attended by Darrell Prose, John Hill, and the Treasurer. Ms. Woodriff confirmed she had never previously seen these documents. While she does not dispute their validity, she has requested that a licensed surveyor identify the centerline of the easement and confirm the bearing on the ground. If the survey confirms what the documents describe, Ms. Woodriff has indicated she would be amenable to incorporating the easement into her current deed — representing the first concrete path to resolving an issue that has gone unaddressed for over fifty years.
