Member Information — Governance Review & Supporting Documentation

This page was prepared to provide members of the Bridgepoint Ditch Company with a factual record of governance matters raised during the Annual Meeting of February 28, 2026, and at the Board Meeting of February 26, 2026. All statements below are supported by documents, correspondence, and meeting records, which are available for review in each section.

The purpose of this page is to allow every member to review the relevant information at their own pace before the upcoming membership meeting. No conclusions are drawn here. The documents speak for themselves, and the decisions belong to the membership.

A membership meeting is scheduled for April 11, 2026. There is pending unfinished business from the February 28 meeting that requires membership action. Your attendance or proxy is important.
Corporate Standing
01

Administrative Dissolution

The organization was administratively dissolved by the State of Oregon for failure to file the required annual report. The company operated without legal standing for approximately two years before reinstatement was completed. The bylaws assign the filing of annual reports and corporate records to the President.
2 documents — State records/reinstatement filing, full board correspondence over 10 months period
Reinstated
02

Loss of Federal Tax-Exempt Status

The organization's 501(c)(12) tax-exempt status was automatically revoked by the IRS after no Form 990-N was filed since the EIN was assigned in March 2020. The Bylaws assign the filing of legal and regulatory compliance to the President. A Form 1024 application for reinstatement has been filed and the $600 fee has been paid. Reinstatement is expected within six to seven months.
3 documents — Board email correspondence, Form 1024 filing, payment receipt
Reinstatement pending
Financial Administration
03

Member Invoicing

Member invoices for 2026 were produced and mailed by the President without authorization from the Treasurer. The invoices contained calculation errors, omitted outstanding balances, deleted deliquent members invoice from Treasurer's dropbox files, and directed member payments to the President's personal address. The bylaws assign invoicing exclusively to the Treasurer and require payments to be sent directly to the Treasurer.
2 documents — bylaws references, email correspondence
Unresolved
04

Bank Account & Financial Controls

The President has been the sole active signatory on the organization's bank account. The bylaws require two signatories — the President and the Secretary. The Secretary confirmed at the February 26 board meeting that he never added his signature in over a year of service. Checks have been processed with a single signature. Additionally, a former officer's signature remains on the account and has not been removed following the transition to the current board.
3 documents — Bylaws provisions, board meeting record
Unresolved
05

Delinquent Account — Mishandled Payment

A member's assessment check was given to the President in April 2025. The check was never deposited or delivered to the Treasurer. It remained on the President's desk for approximately ten months until it became stale. The President acknowledged this at the February 26 board meeting and offered to cover the resulting penalty fee, calling it his mistake.
2 documents — Board meeting record, bylaws reference
Payment since resolved
Maintenance & Oversight
06

Maintenance Expenditures & Practices

Annual maintenance costs have averaged $4,935 against approximately $4,769 in assessment revenue, creating a structural deficit. Maintenance has been authorized and paid by the President without documented board pre-approval as required by the bylaws. There is no written contract or documented scope of work with the contractor. There is no maintenance log tracking which sections of the ditch have been cleaned, making it impossible to verify efficient use of member funds. Contractor invoices have been addressed to the President personally rather than to the organization. The bylaws require ditch banks to remain easily accessible and unobstructed for maintenance — in multiple locations, mounds of excavated material left along the banks have been colonized by blackberries, making those sections impassable. Additionally, the organization has been cleaning and paying for maintenance on a section that crosses Bureau of Land Management property. BLM does not pay assessments. No action has been taken to contact the BLM office in Grants Pass to discuss cost-sharing or responsibility for maintenance on federal land.
5 documents — Financial records, invoices, bylaws references, photos
Unresolved
07

Gate Incident & Contingency Failure — June 8, 2025

On June 8, 2025, the main gate controlling water flow into the ditch was found barely open, restricting delivery to members. The President was out of state at the time. When contacted by phone, the President repeatedly denied the gate was restricted despite being informed by a board member standing directly in front of it. A witness, Darrell Prose, was present and can confirm the condition of the gate. The Treasurer requested that keys to the locked gate be distributed to all board members as a contingency measure to ensure continuous water delivery — the bylaws require the President to guarantee the continuous functioning of gates and filters. This request has never been addressed. Three days after the incident, water levels rose to a point that caused flooding on the neighboring Bottroff property. According to the new property owner, the President did not respond promptly to the flooding report.
3 documents — Video of gate, text message records, witness account
Unresolved
Records & Governance
07

Meeting Minutes & Secretary Duties

The Secretary stated at the opening of the February 26 board meeting that when he took office, he did not read the bylaws or his duties, and that for two years he relied on the other officers to run the ditch. One of the Secretary duties is to produce and record minutes. Board and annual meeting minutes are the official written record of decisions made, actions taken, and matters discussed at each meeting. For a mutual irrigation company, they serve as the authoritative record of board authorization for expenditures, officer duties, and membership resolutions — and are required to be available for review by any member upon request. It is worth noting that the 2025 board meeting minutes were only uploaded to the shared company folder in a rush by the Secretary following the Treasurer's formal notice of February 22, 2026. The February 26, 2026 board meeting minutes remain in draft form — incomplete, unapproved, and largely inaccurate. To date, there are no approved minutes for the February 26 board meeting, no approved or draft minutes for the improperly noticed annual meeting of February 28, and no approved or draft minutes for the board maintenance meeting held on March 7, 2026. The absence of these records leaves the organization without a verifiable account of decisions made, commitments acknowledged, and actions authorized.
4 documents — Secretary's draft minutes, correction request, bylaws references
Under review

Board Communication & Decision-Making

The Secretary confirmed at the February 26 board meeting that he responded to the President's email requesting a board meeting, but sent his reply only to the President and did not include the Treasurer. The decision to take over the Treasurer's invoicing function was made in a private conversation between the President and Secretary, with no meeting notice, no minutes, and no involvement of the Treasurer.

Infrastructure
09

Easement — Progress After Fifty Years

The organization has operated for decades without a recorded easement for access to critical ditch infrastructure, relying instead on informal goodwill from a neighboring landowner. Securing this easement has been identified as a priority since the current board took office over five years ago, but no concrete steps were taken until recently. Through research into county records, the original deed containing the easement description and legal centerline has been located. Darrell Prose, John Hill, and Slim Ameziani have been working directly with Shirley Woodrif, the current landowner. Conversations have been productive — she does not deny the easement exists and has expressed willingness to review a survey. Her deed does not currently reference the easement, and further investigation is needed to understand why the county recorded her deed without it and how the easement can be added to her title for permanent, documented access to Bridgepoint Ditch Company infrastructure. A surveyor will be needed to identify the easement on the ground. The membership will be asked to consider funding for the survey and legal documentation at the next meeting.
5 documents — Deed records, surveyor maps, easement legal description, correspondence
In progress
