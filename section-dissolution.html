Corporate Standing  ·  01 of 09

Administrative Dissolution

Reinstated
Summary

The organization was administratively dissolved by the State of Oregon for failure to file the required annual report. The company operated without legal standing for approximately two years before reinstatement was completed. The bylaws assign the filing of annual reports and corporate records to the President.

Legal References
Bylaws
Article V, Section 1(f)
"The President shall file and maintain up-to-date records of all corporate actions, including but not limited to the annual report with the Secretary of State of Oregon, liens with the county clerk, and any other administrative requirements essential to the good standing and compliance of the organization."

Oregon Revised Statutes
ORS 65.787 — Annual Report
Requires nonprofit corporations to file an annual report with the Oregon Secretary of State. Failure to file within 45 days of the due date may result in administrative dissolution.
ORS 65.357 — Fiduciary Duty of Directors
Directors must act in good faith, with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances, and in a manner the director reasonably believes to be in the best interests of the corporation.
