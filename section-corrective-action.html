Corrective Action Proposal — Governance Modernization

Proposed
Summary

The issues documented in the preceding sections are not isolated incidents. They reflect a pattern of governance that operated without transparency, accountability, or member access to information. Correcting this requires structural changes — not just changes in personnel.

The following proposals use free or near-free tools to modernize how Bridgepoint Ditch Company communicates with its members, conducts meetings, tracks finances and maintenance, and ensures that every member has access to the information they are entitled to. Total estimated annual cost: $20.

These proposals will be presented to the membership for consideration. The decisions belong to the members.

Proposals
01 Recorded Meetings via Zoom $20/year
Problem

Members who cannot attend meetings have no way to know what was discussed or decided. There is no objective record of proceedings. Disputes about what was said go unresolved.

Solution

Host the annual membership meeting on Zoom Pro ($20 for one month, cancel after). Record the meeting. Upload the recording to an unlisted YouTube video — free, unlimited storage, accessible only by link. Share the link with all members afterward.

Remote members participate and vote in real time via Zoom. Board meetings can use the free Zoom tier (40-minute limit) or the same Pro subscription month.

What This Achieves

Every member can watch the full meeting at their convenience. Remote members participate live. The recording is a permanent, uneditable record. Disputes about what was said are eliminated. No additional labor — the host clicks "Record."

02 Member Email Distribution List Free
Problem

Members receive information only through mailed notices and word of mouth. There is no efficient way to distribute updates between meetings. Board members have communicated privately, excluding other officers from decisions.

Solution

Create a free email distribution list using Google Groups. All official communications — meeting notices, financial updates, maintenance schedules — go through the list. Any board member can send. All members receive. The archive is searchable and permanent. Members who prefer paper notices continue receiving them.

What This Achieves

Instant, documented communication with all members. No more private side channels. Every board member sees every communication. Permanent archive eliminates "I never received the notice" disputes. One-time setup: 30 minutes.

03 Financial Transparency Dashboard Free
Problem

Members have no visibility into the organization's financial position between annual meetings. Assessment revenue, maintenance expenditures, and account balances are known only to the officers handling the money.

Solution

Create a view-only Google Sheet accessible to all members via link. Tabs for annual budget, income, expenses, account balance, and delinquent accounts (anonymized). Updated quarterly by the Treasurer — approximately 15 minutes per update. Annual budget presented and adopted by membership vote.

What This Achieves

Complete financial transparency without printing or mailing reports. Members can verify assessments are collected and spent properly at any time. Budget overruns are visible immediately, not discovered a year later.

04 Permanent Document Archive Free
Problem

Governing documents, meeting minutes, financial records, and correspondence have been stored inconsistently. The Secretary could not locate 2025 board meeting minutes when asked. No central, accessible archive exists.

Solution

Maintain a permanent, password-protected online archive (this website or a shared Google Drive folder) containing: bylaws, all meeting minutes, financial reports, maintenance records, correspondence, and easement documents. Updated after each meeting or significant event. Members receive the access link upon request.

Legal basis: ORS 65.771 requires nonprofit corporations to keep permanent records including minutes and governing documents. ORS 65.774 grants members the right to inspect corporate records.
What This Achieves

Every member has access to every governing document at all times. New members can review the organization's complete history. No single officer controls access. Documents cannot be lost, misplaced, or withheld.

05 Maintenance Documentation & Cleaning Log Free
Problem

There is no written record of what maintenance work has been performed, which sections have been cleaned, or what the contractor's scope of work includes. Expenditures cannot be verified. Some sections are cleaned annually while others are neglected. The organization pays for maintenance on BLM property without cost recovery.

Solution

Before each annual cleaning: two board members walk the ditch together, document conditions with photos, define scope of work in writing. Contractor receives written scope before beginning work. After cleaning: photos of completed work, invoice matched against scope. Log entries include date, section cleaned, contractor, cost, and before/after photos. BLM section tracked separately.

Bylaws basis: Article VII, Section 4 requires all paid maintenance to be pre-approved by the Board. Article IV, Section 8 requires formal consensus among a minimum of two board members for any decision-making process.
What This Achieves

Members can verify assessments are spent on actual work. Year-over-year comparison shows whether costs are reasonable. Written scope prevents contractor disputes. BLM cost exposure is documented and actionable.

06 Remote Voting & Participation Free (included in Zoom)
Problem

Members who cannot attend in person must rely on written proxies. Proxy procedures have been challenged and disputed. Members in other states are effectively excluded. Quorum is difficult to achieve with a geographically dispersed membership.

Solution

Allow Zoom attendance with live voting. Members attending via Zoom are counted for quorum. Voting by remote members conducted by roll call — each member states their vote audibly on the recorded meeting. Written proxies remain available for members who cannot attend live or by Zoom.

Legal basis: ORS 65.205 authorizes participation in meetings by remote communication for Oregon nonprofit corporations. A bylaw amendment would formally adopt this provision.
What This Achieves

Members in other states or with scheduling conflicts participate fully. Higher quorum without requiring physical presence. The recorded meeting captures every vote for the permanent record. Proxy disputes are reduced — members vote for themselves in real time.

07 Annual Officer Compliance Report Free
Problem

There is no mechanism to verify that officers are performing their duties between annual meetings. The administrative dissolution, loss of tax-exempt status, financial control failures, and absence of maintenance documentation all went undetected for years.

Solution

Each officer completes a one-page compliance checklist before the annual meeting and presents it in five minutes. The President reports on state and federal filings, maintenance completed, gate inspections, and contingency planning. The Secretary reports on minutes, document maintenance, and bank signatory status. The Treasurer reports on invoicing, collections, delinquent accounts, and financial statements. The checklist is filed in the permanent archive.

What This Achieves

Members see at a glance whether officers performed their duties. Failures become visible immediately, not after years of accumulation. Officers who do their jobs get public credit. New officers have a clear roadmap of what is expected. No additional labor — officers are already required to perform these duties.

08 Electronic Dues Payment via Zelle Free
Problem

Members currently pay assessments by mailing personal checks. Checks have been lost, held for months without deposit, or mailed to the wrong address. The process is slow, error-prone, and creates disputes about whether and when payment was received.

Solution

Enable Zelle on the Bridgepoint Ditch Company's U.S. Bank business account. U.S. Bank offers Zelle at no cost to small business checking account holders. All it requires is for an authorized signatory to log into U.S. Bank online banking or the mobile app, navigate to Transfer & Pay, and enroll with an email address or phone number dedicated to the organization. Members who use Zelle through their own bank can then send assessment payments directly to the Bridgepoint account using the organization's Zelle tag, email, or phone number — no account numbers shared.

Payments arrive within minutes and are deposited directly into the organization's bank account. The Treasurer includes the Zelle payment option on each invoice alongside the traditional mailing address. Members who prefer to pay by check continue doing so.

Setup: One-time enrollment by a bank signatory through U.S. Bank online banking or mobile app. Takes approximately 5 minutes. No fees from U.S. Bank or Zelle. Contact U.S. Bank customer support at 800-872-2657 if Zelle is not visible in your account.
What This Achieves

Members can pay assessments instantly from their phone. No check to write, no envelope to mail, no waiting for deposit. Payments are timestamped and traceable — eliminating disputes about when payment was sent or received. Checks can no longer sit on someone's desk for ten months. Members who travel or live out of state can pay without delay. The Treasurer has immediate confirmation of payment in the bank account.

These proposals require no special expertise, no significant funding, and no increase in volunteer labor. They use tools that are free, widely available, and accessible from any phone or computer.
