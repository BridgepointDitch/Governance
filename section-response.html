Response to Recent Correspondence

Context

Following the distribution of governance documentation to members, a letter was circulated by the President containing a number of assertions regarding the Treasurer's conduct and responsibilities. The following addresses those assertions with reference to the bylaws, IRS definitions, and recordings of official board and membership meetings.

All audio clips below are excerpts from recordings of official Bridgepoint Ditch Company meetings. Transcripts are provided beneath each clip for clarity.

Claim from President's Letter
"The IRS 990-N: Filing is the Treasurer's responsibility — not the President's. At the February 28 meeting, Slim told the membership that organizations under $50,000 in revenue were exempt from filing. That is not correct, and the tax-exempt status had already been lost due to his years of non filing. He has since filed for reinstatement and paid $600 fee, which needed to be done — but this was his obligation from the day he became Treasurer in 2021, his error to own, and his mischaracterization to answer, not mine."

Audio — President at February 28 Annual Meeting
The following recording is from the February 28, 2026 annual meeting. The President references Form 990-EZ as the applicable IRS filing requirement for the organization. Form 990-EZ applies to organizations with gross receipts between $50,000 and $200,000. Bridgepoint Ditch Company collects under $50,000 annually and is therefore not subject to Form 990-EZ. The applicable filing is Form 990-N. The Treasurer's statement at the meeting was correct.
Open Audio
Transcript
"Slim, as treasurer, is supposed to submit a form IRS, 990EZ, I believe, to keep our non profit status. I have not seen any proof of that, period. 990EZ which is our non profit status to be filed by the Treasurer."
For reference: Form 990-EZ is required for organizations with gross receipts between $50,000 and $200,000. Form 990-N (the e-Postcard) is required for organizations with gross receipts under $50,000. Bridgepoint Ditch Company falls in the latter category. The President cited the wrong form. The Treasurer correctly identified that Form 990-EZ did not apply.
