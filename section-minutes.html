Meeting Minutes & Secretary Duties

Under review
Summary

The Secretary stated at the opening of the February 26 board meeting that when he took office, he did not read the bylaws or his duties, and that for two years he relied on the other officers to guide him. Board meeting minutes from 2025 could not be located by the Secretary when asked.

Minutes from the February 26 board meeting were submitted to the board for approval but contained material omissions — several key statements and admissions made during the meeting were not reflected in the Secretary's draft. A detailed list of corrections was submitted by the Treasurer.

Legal References
Bylaws
Article V, Section 2(a)
"The Secretary shall accurately record and maintain minutes of all meetings, ensuring a comprehensive and organized record of discussions, decisions, and actions taken."

Bylaw screenshot: docs/screenshots/bylaws-art5-sec2a.png

Article V, Section 2(d)
"The Secretary shall diligently uphold a current and precise record of the organization's bylaws, policies, and other governing documents. Additionally, the Secretary is entrusted with maintaining secure documentation of passwords and usernames for various online applications utilized by the company."
Article IV, Section 10
"Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised will govern all meetings of the organization."
Robert's Rules of Order
RONR 48:1-5 — Content and Preparation of Minutes
Minutes are a record of what was done at a meeting. The secretary is responsible for preparing and maintaining accurate minutes of all proceedings.
RONR 48:7 — Accuracy of Minutes
Minutes should contain a record of what was done at the meeting. Material omissions from the minutes compromise the integrity of the organizational record.
Oregon Revised Statutes
ORS 65.771 — Corporate Records
Nonprofit corporations are required to keep permanent records including minutes of all meetings of members and the board of directors, as well as records of all actions taken by committees of the board.
