Corporate Standing  ·  02 of 09

Loss of Federal Tax-Exempt Status

Reinstatement pending
Summary

The organization's 501(c)(12) tax-exempt status was automatically revoked by the IRS after no Form 990-N was filed since the EIN was assigned in March 2020. The Bylaws assign the filing of legal and regulatory compliance to the President. A Form 1024 application for reinstatement has been filed and the $600 fee has been paid. Reinstatement is expected within six to seven months.

Legal References
Bylaws
Article V, Section 1(g)
"The President, in collaboration with the Board of Directors, shall ensure that the organization adheres to all legal and regulatory obligations, proactively addressing administrative requirements to maintain the Company's standing in accordance with applicable laws."
Article V, Section 1(f)
"The President shall file and maintain up-to-date records of all corporate actions, including but not limited to the annual report with the Secretary of State of Oregon, liens with the county clerk, and any other administrative requirements essential to the good standing and compliance of the organization."
Federal Law
IRC Section 6033(j) — Automatic Revocation
Tax-exempt status is automatically revoked for any organization that fails to file required returns or notices for three consecutive years.
IRC Section 501(c)(12) — Mutual Ditch or Irrigation Companies
Provides tax-exempt status for mutual ditch or irrigation companies whose income consists solely of assessments, dues, and fees collected from members for the purpose of meeting expenses.
IRS Form 990-N (e-Postcard)
Required annual filing for tax-exempt organizations with gross receipts normally $50,000 or less. No Form 990-N has been filed since the EIN was assigned in March 2020.
